Food-Habits-Tendencies

​Modern city-dweller is used to consider his food for granted, almost like the air we breathe. We all understand that nutrition is a vital need, everybody feels hunger sometimes, but struggling for food usually changed to the choice of it in a menu, fridge or grocery shelves. But things​ ​ were​ ​ not​ ​ so​ ​ smooth​ ​ always. Control over the food sources always was a factor of prosperity because of constantly high demand but limited amount. Food was associated with power, prosperity and, unfortunately, sometimes weapon. Farming was the main occupation for a vast majority of mankind. Despite it’s vital meaning food production barely got any development at all. From ancient times after very invention of irrigation, the plow, and other basic facilities, progress seemed to stuck. As food exchange was concerned with problems of preserving freshness and large costs, nutrition​ ​ of​ ​ civilization​ ​ consisted​ ​ mostly​ ​ of​ ​ locally-produced​ ​ food​ ​ and​ ​ traditional​ ​ meals. Therefore ​different nature conditions formed different food habits, determining the shape of peoples cultures themselves​. Neighbouring nomadic livestock tribes and settled agricultural​ ​ workers​ ​ shared​ ​ less​ ​ similarity​ ​ than​ ​ their​ ​ “colleagues”​ ​ from​ ​ other​ ​ parts​ ​ of​ ​ the​ ​ world. Exploration and analysis of world food habits may help in better understanding of food security and be useful in finding new ways of solving this problems.

Data

Main dataset used in this project was taken from OECD website Part of the data we used can be found here.

Approach

Approach Analysing nutritional habits requires unsupervised approach, so we used machine learning method - clustering. Namely, k-means and DBSCAN algorithms. We also applied PCA and t-SNE dimensionality reduction to visualize high-dimensional data.
